Auckland

Description: Auckland, dubbed the "City of Sails",is the largest metropolitan center in New Zealand. The city blends modern urban life with natural surroundings, flanked by harbors and dormant volcanoes. Its attractions focus on bustling harbor life and spectacular city panoramas.

Popular Activities: Visit the Sky Tower for a 360-degree view. Enjoy dining and nightlife at Viaduct Harbour. Explore the underwater world at SEA LIFE Kelly Tarlton's Aquarium. Shop in the trendy Ponsonby area.

Rotorua (North Island)

Description:Rotorua is the heart of geothermal activity and Māori culture. The city is famous for its sulfurous aroma and active geological sites. It is the best place to experience the rich Māori cultural heritage and see unique natural wonders.

Popular Activities: Witness boiling mud pools and geysers at Wai-O-Tapu Thermal Wonderland. Enjoy an authentic Māori cultural experience, including performances and a hāngi (earth oven) meal, at Mitai Māori Village or Te Puia.

Matamata (Waikato, North Island)

Description: Matamata is a rural area that is home to the iconic Hobbiton Movie Set. With its beautiful green hills and peaceful atmosphere, this location transports visitors directly to the fantasy world of Middle-earth from The Lord of the Rings and The Hobbit films.

Popular Activities:Take a guided tour of the Hobbiton Movie Set, pose in front of the unique Hobbit holes, and enjoy a ginger beer at The Green Dragon Inn.

Queenstown (South Island)

Description: Queenstown is known as the "Adventure Capital of the World". Dramatically situated on the shore of Lake Wakatipu and surrounded by the magnificent Remarkables Mountains, it is a haven for extreme sports enthusiasts and outdoor activities. Its scenery is among the most stunning globally.

Popular Activities: Try extreme activities like Bungee Jumping (at Kawarau Bridge) and Jet Boating on the river. Ride the Skyline Queenstown gondola for luge and breathtaking views. Take a historic steamship cruise on Lake Wakatipu aboard the TSS Earnslaw.

Christchurch (South Island)

Description: Christchurch, or the "Garden City", is the largest city in the South Island, famous for its well-maintained parks, historic architecture combined with modern design, and the tranquil Avon River. The city offers a more relaxed and cultured atmosphere.

Popular Activities: Enjoy Avon River Punting (a traditional boat ride) on the peaceful Avon River. Explore the natural beauty of the Christchurch Botanic Gardens. Visit the Canterbury Museum to learn about local history.

Tekapo (Mackenzie Region, South Island)

Description: Tekapo is located in the renowned Mackenzie Basin. The area has a dual appeal: the stunning turquoise blue lake water, caused by glacial melt, and its status as part of the Aoraki Mackenzie Dark Sky Reserve—one of the best places in the world for stargazing.

Popular Activities: Visit the iconic Church of the Good Shepherd on the lake edge. Go stargazing (astronomy) at night, where the Milky Way is clearly visible.
